Newton's Second Law of Motion

The acceleration of an object as produced by a net force is directly proportional to the magnitude of the net force, in the same direction as the net force, and inversely proportional to the mass of the object.

F = m * a

F: force exerted,
m: mass of the object,
a: acceleration produced as a result.

Acceleration is simply the rate of change of velocity, that means by 'how much' will the velocity of the object increase or decrease per unit time.

Linear Dependency Graph

This kind of relationship is called "Linear Dependency". This means as one variable increases, the other by the same factor irrepective of the value of either variable. That means, in this case, whether force is 500N or 2500N, acceleration will increase by same factor of 0.1 (for a constant mass of 10Kg)

Reciprocal Dependency Graph

This kind of relationship is called "Reciprocal Dependency". This means as the independent variable changes, the dependent variable changes by an exponential factor that depends on the value of the independent variable. That means, in this case, as you move from mass 10Kg to 100Kg the change in acceleration will slow down (for a constant force of 2500N)

SUMMARY AND QUICK TEST

To summarize, Newton's second law of motion basically means that acceleration is affected by both the force exerted and the mass of the object. The more force exerted, the more acceleration is produced. The more mass the object has, the lesser acceleration is produced. Thus force and mass kind of balance each other.
